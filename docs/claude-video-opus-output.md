

# Hook

**First 15 seconds — recommended opening sequence:**

FADE FROM BLACK. 1.5 seconds of silence. Then:

Wide shot of a phone lying on a worn wooden counter — the kind you'd find in a kedai runcit. Natural light. A WhatsApp notification slides down. Hold for a beat.

CUT TO: The Sah.Bukti landing page, slow scroll, real browser. The tagline appears letter by letter on screen — not on the website, but typeset clean over black:

**Proof before payment. Clean books after.**

CUT TO: Real dashboard. A single number animating up. Evidence items waiting for review.

This is the entire pitch in 15 seconds: a message comes in, proof exists, the owner is in control.

Pacing: Slow. Leave room. Let the product breathe on screen.

# Music Direction

**Do not use any named tracks.** This is direction for a composer or library search.

**Overall feel:**
Minimal electronic. Sparse. Think a single sustained pad that evolves over two minutes. Not ambient — it should have forward momentum, but restrained. The kind of score where you notice the silence more than the sound.

**Structure:**

- **0:00–0:15:** Silence for 2 seconds, then a single low tone fades in. A soft pulse begins — not a beat, just a rhythmic breath. Barely there.
- **0:15–0:55:** A second layer enters — a clean, high piano note or muted pluck, repeating at wide intervals. The pad beneath it shifts key once. The feeling is: something is being built.
- **0:55–1:15:** The pulse drops out. Just the pad. For the mid-film text beat ("One decision. One record."), near-silence. A single sustained note.
- **1:15–1:45:** The pulse returns, slightly more present. A subtle bass element enters — not heavy, but grounding. Forward motion increases.
- **1:45–2:00:** The layers converge. This is the fullest the music gets — but "full" here means three elements playing together, not a crescendo. Controlled confidence.
- **2:00–2:22:** Everything strips back. The final shot of the phone on the counter plays over a single held chord that decays naturally. The last 4 seconds are silence.

**Reference mood (not tracks):**
The restraint of a Jony Ive product film. The breath of a Muji store. The confidence of knowing you don't need to be loud.

# Edit Notes

**Pacing:**
This film is slower than you think it should be. That's intentional. Every hackathon demo is fast. This one breathes. The judges will notice because it's the only one that trusts its own product enough to hold a shot for five seconds.

**Transitions:**
- Cut to black between major sections (evidence → review, review → invoices). 0.5 second black. Not a fade — a cut to black, hold, cut back in.
- Within sections, direct cuts. No dissolves. No slides. No zooms except the two subtle push-ins noted in the shot list.

**Screen recordings:**
- Record at the highest resolution the product supports. 2x if retina.
- Use a clean cursor (macOS default, no custom cursors).
- Mouse movements should be slow, deliberate, human. Not robotic. Not rushed. The mouse should move like someone who knows exactly where they're going.
- If the product has loading states, include them. Real products load. That's not a flaw — it's proof this is real.

**Color grade:**
- Slightly desaturated. Lift the blacks by 5%. Subtle warmth in the midtones. The goal is to make screen recordings feel cinematic without altering the product's actual colors. The product should look like itself — just in better light.

**The counter shot (shots 2 and 14):**
- This is the emotional anchor. Same location, same framing, same light. The only difference is what's on the phone screen. Beginning: a WhatsApp notification (the problem). End: the Sah.Bukti dashboard (the solution). The audience should feel the transformation without being told.
- If possible, shoot this on a real counter in a real kedai. Natural light from a window. No styling. Let the texture of the wood and the wear on the counter do the work. This is not aspirational — this is where the product actually lives.

**Runtime discipline:**
- Do not exceed 2:30. If anything feels padded, cut it. The film should feel like it ended one shot before you expected. Leave the judges wanting to see more, not relieved it's over.

**The single most important thing:**
Every frame must show real product or real typography on black. Nothing else. No illustrations. No icons. No diagrams. No "how it works" slides. The product is the proof. That's the whole point.
