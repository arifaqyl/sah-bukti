Sah.Bukti helps Malaysian micro-sellers control payments and collections without changing how they already work.

Most of these businesses already run from WhatsApp.

Orders come in through chat.
Payment messages come in through chat.
Receipts, screenshots, and proof get passed around in chat.
Then at month-end, everything has to be cleaned up manually.

That is the problem Sah.Bukti solves.

Sah.Bukti is a WhatsApp-first collections control plane for Malaysian micro-sellers.

This is the Sah.Bukti landing page.

The website is not trying to replace WhatsApp.
WhatsApp stays the daily workflow.
The website gives the owner a clean control surface for review, visibility, and export.

This is the login and onboarding flow.

The owner sets up the business once, then starts using the system through the same tools they already depend on.

After setup, this is the dashboard.

The dashboard shows what is pending, what is approved, and what still needs attention.
It gives the owner a real operating picture instead of relying on memory and scattered messages.

Now we go into the core workflow.

A customer sends an order in plain text through WhatsApp, the same way they normally would.

For example, the customer says they want two nasi lemak.

Sah.Bukti captures that message and turns it into a structured invoice draft.

So instead of manually copying chat messages into a spreadsheet, or trying to remember what was ordered, the message becomes a proper business record.

Now the seller has a real invoice with a customer reference, items, amount, and payment status.

This is the evidence flow.

Messy input comes in first.
Then Sah.Bukti normalizes it into something reviewable.

Next, the customer sends a payment message through WhatsApp.

This is the most important part of the product.

Sah.Bukti does not auto-confirm the payment.

Instead, it creates a payment proof and places it in the review queue.

That means chat can submit evidence, but chat does not decide financial truth.

This is the review page.

The review queue is the trust boundary.

Here, the owner sees what came in, which invoice it belongs to, the amount, the source, and what still needs confirmation.

Only after the owner approves does the system update ledger truth.

That approval step is the core of Sah.Bukti.

It protects the business from messy evidence, wrong assumptions, and false confirmation.

So the product is fast because it works with WhatsApp, but it is also safe because approval remains the single gate before truth.

Once approved, the invoice status updates properly.

This is the invoices view.

Now the business is no longer relying on scattered chat fragments.
It has clean, structured, reviewable records with a real payment state.

This is the customers view.

Customer records stay connected to the transaction trail, so follow-up is easier and more consistent.

This is the inventory section.

Inventory exists, but it is not the core story.
The core story is collections control and proof before payment truth.

This is the readiness view.

At month-end, Sah.Bukti gives the owner a clearer view of what is actually ready, what is still risky, and what still needs action.

And finally, this is the export flow.

Instead of chasing screenshots, old chat messages, and memory, the owner gets a cleaner export and a clearer accountant handoff.

So the full Sah.Bukti loop is:

WhatsApp message,
evidence capture,
review queue,
owner approval,
ledger update,
and export.

Sah.Bukti is not a generic chatbot.
It is not a full accounting suite.
And it is not just another invoice form.

It is a collections control plane built around how Malaysian micro-sellers already operate.

Forward messy evidence in.
Review it.
Approve what is true.
Clean books after.

That is Sah.Bukti.
